📖 Help & Guide

🚀 Quick Start
  1. Click anywhere on the map — or use 📍 My Location in the sidebar — to set your shooting spot.
  2. Pick a date and drag the time slider to preview the sky at any moment.
  3. Switch tabs (Sun & Moon, Milky Way, Calculators) for detailed data and charts.
  4. Tap 📡 AR Sky to open the live AR view and point your phone at the sky.
🗺️ Planner Tab
  • Map click → sets your location and updates all overlays.
  • 🎯 Set Target → click a second point (building, mountain, horizon). The alignment line and distance appear on the map.
  • Layer buttons (Street / Satellite / Terrain) change the basemap.
  • 🌐 Sky View → polar sky dome showing sun & moon arcs and Milky Way direction for the selected time.
  • 🔍 Find Alignment → searches a date range for moments when the sun or moon rises/sets along a specific azimuth (great for Manhattanhenge-style shots).
  • ⭐ Favorites → save and reload named locations.
☀️ Sun & Moon Tab
  • Shows precise rise/set, golden hour, blue hour, civil/nautical/astronomical twilight times.
  • Directions card: drag the time picker to see live azimuth & altitude for any moment of the day.
  • Moon data includes phase, illumination %, age and next full/new moon dates.
🌌 Milky Way Tab
  • Shows Galactic Center rise, transit, set times and its current azimuth & altitude.
  • Visibility bar rates conditions based on moon illumination and horizon altitude.
  • Best shooting window is shown when Galactic Center is above the horizon and moon is below.
📐 Calculators Tab
  • Depth of Field — sensor size, focal length, aperture, subject distance → near/far limits and hyperfocal distance.
  • 500 / NPF Rule — maximum exposure before stars trail for your lens and sensor.
  • Long-exposure equivalents — ND filter stop calculator.
  • Timelapse — interval, duration, frame rate → clip length.
  • Field of View — sensor + focal length → horizontal, vertical and diagonal FOV in degrees.
  • Star Trails — shutter / total rotation arc calculator.
📡 Live AR View
  • Opens the rear camera with sky objects (sun, moon, planets, Milky Way, paths) overlaid in real time.
  • Requires HTTPS, camera permission, and motion-sensor permission.
  • Compass signal: on Android, Chrome may need Motion Sensors enabled.
    Go to Chrome ⋮ → Settings → Site settings → Motion sensors → Allow.
  • On iOS, tap the AR button and allow Motion & Orientation Access when prompted.
    If denied: Settings → Safari → Motion & Orientation Access → on.
  • REL badge on the compass ruler means heading is device-relative (not north-locked). Point the phone at a known direction and mentally offset.
  • Use layer buttons (☀ ☽ 🌌 🪐 〰 ⊞) to toggle overlays. Adjust FOV slider to match your lens.
  • Date/time strip at the top lets you preview any future sky without leaving AR mode.
🔍 Alignment Finder Tips
  • Pin the sun/moon position on the map at the spot where you want the celestial body to appear.
  • Place the Target pin at your camera position on the opposite side of the subject.
  • The tool calculates the exact bearing and searches your date range for matching moments.
  • Narrow the Az Tolerance (±1–2°) for precision shots; widen it (±5°) for a broader search window.
💾 Offline / PWA
  • Install the app via your browser's Add to Home Screen option for offline use.
  • All core features work without internet. Map tiles are cached on first load.
  • Weather and geocoding (address search) require a live connection.

PhotoPlanner

Version 1.0.0

A photographer's planning tool for the sun, moon, Milky Way, and celestial alignments — works fully offline as a PWA.

☀️ Full solar & lunar ephemeris — golden hour, twilight, phase
🌌 Milky Way Galactic Center rise/transit/set with visibility rating
📡 Live AR sky overlay — sun, moon, planets, Milky Way on camera
🔍 Alignment finder — Manhattanhenge-style azimuth search
🌐 Sky dome view — polar projection of the full sky
📐 Photography calculators — DOF, NPF, timelapse, ND, FOV, star trails
💾 Offline-capable PWA with service worker caching
